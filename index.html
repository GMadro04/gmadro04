Introducing myself

I’m a electronics engineering student at University of Nariño, Colombia. Currently, I participate in research activities on swarm robotics that are developed in collaboration with IRIDIA, Université Libre de Bruxelles, Belgium. With focus on collective behaviors and data analysis using ARGoS3 and Python. In the past, I also participated in projects related to signal processing and robot programming and simulation with ROS and Gazebo, i was undergraduate research assistant in the Servicio Geológico Colombiano (SGC) - Observatorio vulcanológico y sismológico de Pasto.
My research focuses mainly on study behavior-based design methods and design of control software for swarm robots. Read more about my research here.

Researches

Empirical evaluation of scalability, flexibility and robustness of typical collective behaviors for robot swarms.

On going


The swarm robotics literature has a significant gap in terms of research work that compares collective behaviors under the same experimental design. The objective of this work is to create a repository of collective behaviors and to develop an experimental protocol, both aimed at facilitating the study of robot swarm properties. These tools hold significant value as they promote standardization within this field of study.
